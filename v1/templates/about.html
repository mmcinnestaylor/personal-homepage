About

Before studying Computer Science I received a B.A. in Philosophy from the University of Florida. I subsequently spent several years as an internal auditor at a financial institution, while also working as an IT contractor in my free time.

At Florida State University I study Computer Science and Applied Mathematics, pursuing B.S. degrees in both areas. I completed an undergraduate thesis under the supervision of Dr. Sonia Haiduc, focusing on performing skin lesion classification using the Inception v3 CNN architecture. I am a member of FSU USER Lab, instructed by Dr. Chris Mills. Our research focuses on automating software tracibility link recovery. I also serve as an officer of FSU's ACM student chapter.
